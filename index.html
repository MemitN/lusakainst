Our Story

Who We Are

Lusaka Institute of Science and Technology was established to address the growing demand for skilled technical professionals in Kenya. Located in Narok County, the institute has grown to become a leading provider of technical and vocational education.

We offer a broad portfolio of programmes across ICT, Social and Health Sciences, Tourism and Hospitality, and Business Studies. All programmes are nationally accredited and aligned with industry needs.

Our dedicated faculty of qualified lecturers, modern facilities, and strong industry partnerships create an environment where students thrive academically and professionally.

Our Vision

To be the leading technical and vocational institute in Eastern Africa, producing graduates who are globally competitive, morally upright and technologically empowered to drive sustainable development.

Our Mission

To offer quality, accessible and relevant technical education and training that equips learners with practical skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to excel in their careers and positively impact society.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

Excellence

We uphold the highest standards in teaching, learning and administration, consistently striving for exceptional outcomes.

Integrity

We conduct all activities with transparency, honesty and ethical responsibility, building trust with students, staff, partners and community.

h3>Inclusivity

Community Service

Academic Requirements

Entry Requirements

Diploma Programmes

  • KCSE with a minimum aggregate grade of D+ (Plus)
  • For competitive programmes (ICT, Health): C- and above is preferred
  • Relevant subject grades as specified per programme
  • Certificate of Good Conduct (for health-related programmes)
  • Kenya Equivalency Certificate for holders of foreign qualifications

Certificate Programmes

  • KCSE minimum aggregate of D (Plain)
  • KCPE holders may apply for bridging courses
  • Mature-age entry: 25 years and above may apply without formal qualifications
  • National ID or Birth Certificate (original + copy)
  • Recent passport-size photographs (4 copies)

KASNEB Professional Courses

  • KCSE minimum C- for CPA and CS programmes
  • ATC: D+ acceptable as entry level
  • Holders of relevant certificates may be exempted from some papers
  • Registered students must be enrolled at an approved tuition centre

Short Courses

  • Open to all — no minimum academic qualification required
  • Basic literacy in English and numeracy expected
  • Computer basics required for advanced ICT short courses
  • All applicants must be 16 years of age or older

Our Partners & Accreditation Bodies

Lusaka Institute works with leading national bodies, regulatory authorities and industry partners to deliver quality education.

Accreditation & Regulatory Bodies

Official Accreditation Bodies

These organisations regulate and certify our programmes, ensuring Lusaka graduates hold nationally recognised qualifications.

KNEC

Examinations Body

The Kenya National Examinations Council is the primary examination body for technical and vocational qualifications in Kenya. KNEC administers examinations for all our diploma and certificate programmes.

www.knec.ac.ke ↗

KASNEB

Professional Accountancy Body

Kenya Accountants and Secretaries National Examinations Board (KASNEB) registers and examines students for professional qualifications including CPA, ATC, CS and CIFA. Lusaka is an approved tuition centre.

www.kasneb.or.ke ↗

GRETSA

Secretarial Registration Body

The Government Registered Technical and Secretarial Association accredits secretarial programmes at Lusaka, qualifying graduates for professional secretarial roles across public and private sectors.

Contact Us for Details

Ludepe Teachers

Teaching Standards & Pedagogy

Ludepe Teachers provides professional standards and pedagogical support for our faculty. Through this partnership, Lusaka lecturers receive continuous professional development ensuring high-quality teaching.

Contact Us for Details

TVETA

Technical & Vocational Authority

The Technical and Vocational Education and Training Authority (TVETA) is the government regulator for TVET institutions in Kenya. Lusaka Institute is registered under the TVETA framework.

www.tveta.go.ke ↗

Ministry of Health

Health Sciences Partnership

Our Social and Health Sciences faculty collaborates with the Ministry of Health Kenya for student attachments in public health facilities, ensuring real clinical and community health experience.

www.health.go.ke ↗

KATA / KATO

Tourism Industry Bodies

The Kenya Association of Tour Agents (KATA) and Kenya Association of Tour Operators (KATO) partner with our Tourism faculty to provide industry exposure, attachments and certification.

Contact for Details

IHRM

Human Resource Body

The Institute of Human Resource Management (IHRM) provides professional recognition for our Diploma in Human Resource Management graduates, enabling them to register as graduate members.

www.ihrm.or.ke ↗

HELB

Higher Education Loans Board

Lusaka is an approved institution for the Higher Education Loans Board (HELB). Eligible students can apply for government-backed student loans to support tuition and accommodation costs.

www.helb.co.ke ↗
